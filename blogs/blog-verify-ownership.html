Real estate fraud remains a serious issue in Pakistan. Many buyers fall victim to fake ownership claims, duplicate files, or illegal constructions. At Lateef Properties, we believe every investment should begin with complete ownership verification to ensure your security and peace of mind.

Step-by-Step Guide to Verify Property Ownership in Pakistan

1. Confirm the Original Title Documents

Ask the seller for original property documents such as the Sale Deed, Allotment Letter, and Lease Agreement. Cross-check the title number and property size for accuracy.

2. Verify from the Local Land Record Authority

Each province has its own land records department. For Karachi, you can verify ownership through the Sindh Board of Revenue or KDA (Karachi Development Authority).

  • ✔️ Visit the official Sindh Zameen Portal.
  • ✔️ Enter the property number or CNIC to view registered details.

3. Cross-Check with Society or Builder

If the property lies within a private housing scheme like DHA Karachi or Bahria Town, visit the developer’s office to verify records directly.

4. Check for Legal Dues or Encumbrances

Ensure there are no outstanding loans, utility bills, or court disputes linked to the property. Ask for a No Objection Certificate (NOC) before purchase.
